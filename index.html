What Angel does

Angel watches for patterns, not keywords.

Screen-time limits and content filters can help, but many serious risks develop through pressure, secrecy, manipulation, exclusion, or harmful escalation. Angel is built to notice those dynamics over time on compatible devices.

Understands context

Angel is designed to understand what is visible on screen, who is involved, and how an interaction is changing.

Remembers patterns

People, topics, apps, and repeated signals can matter more than any single message or page.

Alerts with care

Parents should not see everything. Angel is designed to raise a sparse, explainable signal when attention may be needed.

How it works

A calm safety layer for screen-native childhood.

01

See the screen context

Angel starts from what ordinary tools often miss: visible screen activity across supported environments.

02

Look for concerning dynamics

The product is built around behavioral signals: pressure, secrecy, coordinated exclusion, boundary pushing, and harmful escalation.

03

Escalate only when it matters

Angel's parent experience is designed around high-threshold, explainable alerts rather than constant surveillance.

The parent experience

A useful signal, not another stream to monitor.

Angel should tell a parent what changed, why it may matter, and what a caring next step could look like. The Friends beta will help tune that experience with real households before a wider launch.

Trust principles

Protection without surveillance culture.

Not a full parent feed

Angel is not designed to show parents every message, click, or moment.

Not a diagnostic tool

Angel surfaces safety signals for human follow-up. It does not label children or make medical conclusions.

Honest coverage

Friends begins with supported environments. The product should be clear about what it can and cannot see.

Built with families

The beta is free because the right product needs parent and child feedback before scale.
