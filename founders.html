Who we are

We are parents who understand AI, families, trust, and the dangers of the internet.

Angel is being built by Luka Crnkovic-Friis and Benjamin Pommeraud: two operators with experience in production AI, consumer technology, gaming platforms, and trust-sensitive products. Our goal is to build a tool that we need for our own children. It is not about surveillance. It is about peace of mind and making sure parents get the right information when they need to act.

Why this team

Children's digital safety needs both deep AI and consumer discipline.

Most parental-control products were built around basic rules that children can bypass: block this site, limit this app, show this log. Angel is built around a harder question: can software understand context well enough to help parents when risk is developing, without turning family life into constant surveillance?

That requires two kinds of experience. The technical experience of people who can build an AI that is reliable, explainable, and careful with uncertainty. And the life experience of people who know what it is to raise kids in today's world.

Luka and Benjamin bring those two sides together.

How we build

Trust is a product requirement.

Angel is being designed around a few simple constraints.

No surveillance

More data is not automatically safer. Angel will not turn parenting into a monitoring job, and it should protect children's privacy.

Sparse alerts

Parents need fewer, clearer signals. Angel should escalate only when the pattern deserves attention.

Explainable context

An alert should say what changed, why it may matter, and what kind of next step might help.

Honest limits

Angel will not be 100% reliable and will not replace parents being parents.

No child labeling

Angel is not a diagnostic tool. It will not replace doctors or educators or put children in boxes.
